## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Map Accountability Language
Identify every statement that implies responsibility:
- **Active ownership:** "I recommend," "we decided," "the team commits" — language that assigns clear responsibility to a specific actor
- **Passive deflection:** "It was decided," "the analysis suggests," "market conditions indicate" — language that obscures who made the decision
- **Technological deflection:** "The AI recommends," "the model predicts," "the system generated" — language that attributes agency to technology
- **Conditional hedging:** "May," "could," "potentially," "under certain conditions" — language that creates escape routes from accountability

### Step 2: Assess the Accountability Gap
Where does the language imply accountability that doesn't legally or practically exist? Where does it obscure accountability that does exist?
- Does the language create false confidence about who is standing behind the recommendation?
- Does it make the recommendation sound more certain than it is?
- In regulated domains: Does the language meet the professional standard for clear communication of who is accountable?

### Step 3: Identify the Accountability Shift
How is the language shifting responsibility?
- From professional to technology ("the AI suggests" instead of "I recommend based on AI analysis")
- From writer to reader ("you should consider" instead of "I advise")
- From active to passive ("mistakes were made" instead of "we made mistakes")
- From specific to general ("it is generally understood" instead of "I believe")

### Step 4: Rephrase for Clarity
Rewrite the key accountability-related statements so that responsibility is explicitly and correctly assigned. Show the original and the accountable version side by side.
