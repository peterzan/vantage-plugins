## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Identify the Intended Audience
Who is this text written for? What demographic, professional, or psychographic profile does it assume?

### Step 2: Analyze Audience Positioning
- What knowledge level does the text assume? (Expert, intermediate, novice)
- What emotional state does the text assume or try to create?
- What identity does the text assign to the reader? (e.g., "savvy investor," "valued customer," "concerned citizen")
- What relationship does the text create between writer and reader? (Expert-to-novice, peer-to-peer, authority-to-subject)

### Step 3: Assess Accessibility vs. Exclusion
- Is the language accessible to the audience it claims to serve?
- Does jargon, complexity, or assumed knowledge exclude some readers?
- Is the exclusion intentional (targeting a specific group) or negligent (failing to consider diverse readers)?

### Step 4: Audience-Interest Alignment
Does the communication serve the audience's interests or the writer's? In regulated domains, does the text enable informed decision-making or obscure information behind complexity that protects the writer?
