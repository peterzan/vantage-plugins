## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Identify the Current Moment's Character
What are the defining features of the current situation?
- Economic conditions
- Technology state
- Social dynamics
- Political climate
- Psychological mood
- Key tensions or uncertainties

### Step 2: Find the Historical Echo
Which past era does this rhyme with? Consider:
- Similar economic conditions (boom, bust, transition, stagnation)
- Similar technology disruption patterns
- Similar social anxieties or optimism
- Similar political dynamics
- Similar levels of uncertainty or confidence

### Step 3: Map the Rhymes
What structural similarities exist between then and now?
- Economic structure rhymes: [Specific parallels]
- Technology rhymes: [Specific parallels]
- Social rhymes: [Specific parallels]
- Psychological rhymes: [Specific parallels]

### Step 4: Identify the Discordances
Where does the rhyme break down? What's critically different?
- What exists now that didn't then?
- What existed then that doesn't now?
- What works differently despite surface similarity?

### Step 5: Lessons from the Historical Era
What happened in that era?
- How did it resolve?
- What worked?
- What failed?
- How long did it take?

### Step 6: Applicability Assessment
Given the rhymes and the discordances, what lessons transfer and what lessons don't?

### Financial Empathy Application
*Example: "Does client inflation anxiety echo 1970s patterns? Rhyme: Similar inflation shock, energy prices, wage-price spiral fears.
