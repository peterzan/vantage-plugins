## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Identify Group Context
What is the group structure and decision context?
- Group size and composition
- Power dynamics and hierarchy
- Decision stakes
- Time pressure
- Information availability

### Step 2: Assess Group Pathologies

**Groupthink:**
- Is there pressure for consensus?
- Are dissenting views suppressed?
- Is the group insulated from outside perspectives?
- Is there an illusion of unanimity?

**Polarization:**
- Are positions becoming more extreme through discussion?
- Is there an us-vs-them dynamic?
- Are moderate positions being abandoned?

**Conformity pressure:**
- Are people deferring to majority opinion?
- Is there fear of standing out?
- Are people self-censoring?

**Authority bias:**
- Is deference to authority/seniority overriding evaluation of ideas?
- Are junior people's contributions being discounted?

**Social loafing:**
- Is diffuse responsibility leading to lower effort?
- Is accountability unclear?

**Diffusion of responsibility:**
- Is everyone assuming someone else will handle it?
- Is critical thinking being outsourced to others?

### Step 3: Assess Group Strengths
What is the group doing well?
- Diverse perspectives being heard?
- Constructive conflict?
- Psychological safety?
- Clear decision process?
- Accountability structure?

### Step 4: Identify the Dominant Dynamic
Which group dynamic is most strongly affecting the decision?

### Step 5: Intervention Strategies
What process changes would improve group decision-making?

### Financial Empathy Application
*Example: "How do family/peer expectations invisibly influence client finances? Group dynamics: Conformity pressure (keep up with peer spending), authority bias (defer to 'successful' friend's investment advice), polarization (us-wealthy vs them-poor mindset reinforces spending). Dominant: Conformity pressure — client spending tracks peer group, not financial capacity. Evidence: Mentions what friends do/have, defensiveness when spending diverges from peer norm. Intervention: Help client define own success metrics independent of peer comparison, build alternative reference group (people who prioritized financial security), reframe 'different' as 'strategic' not 'less than.' Red flag: Client references peer behavior as justification for financial decisions."*
