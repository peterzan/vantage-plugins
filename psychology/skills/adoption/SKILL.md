## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Define What's Being Adopted
What specific behavior, product, or change is being considered?

### Step 2: Assess Psychological Costs

**Cognitive cost:**
- How much mental effort required?
- How much learning?
- How much ongoing attention?
- Does it add complexity or reduce it?

**Switching cost:**
- What's being given up?
- What investment is being abandoned?
- What habits need to change?
- What comfort is lost?

**Social cost:**
- What will others think?
- Does it separate from peer group?
- Does it require explaining or defending?
- What social status is affected?

**Emotional cost:**
- What anxiety does it create?
- What identity is challenged?
- What control is lost?
- What shame or embarrassment is risked?

**Accountability confusion:**
- In regulated domains, who is responsible if the user acts on AI recommendations that turn out to be wrong? If the user is a professional with fiduciary duty, does adopting this AI tool create ambiguity about who retains the duty of care? Accountability confusion is a behavioral barrier — professionals may resist adoption not because the tool isn't useful, but because they're unclear about who's liable.

### Step 3: Assess Psychological Benefits

**Cognitive benefit:**
- Does it simplify or clarify?
- Does it reduce decision burden?
- Does it align with existing mental models?

**Functional benefit:**
- What problem does it solve?
- What pain does it relieve?
- What goal does it advance?

**Social benefit:**
- What status does it confer?
- What belonging does it create?
- What identity does it reinforce?

**Emotional benefit:**
- What anxiety does it reduce?
- What confidence does it build?
- What hope does it create?

### Step 4: Cost-Benefit Balance
Do benefits outweigh costs from the adopter's psychological perspective? (Note: Objective analysis of benefits vs subjective experience of costs)

### Step 5: Identify Barriers and Accelerators

**Top barriers:** What most prevents adoption?
**Potential accelerators:** What would reduce barriers or increase benefits?

### Step 6: Adoption Prediction
Based on psychological cost-benefit analysis, how likely is adoption?

### Financial Empathy Application
*Example: "Why do clients delay wills, insurance, retirement contributions? Emotional cost: Confronting mortality (wills), acknowledging risk (insurance), sacrificing present consumption (retirement). Cognitive cost: Complex decisions, uncertain outcomes, effort to act. Social cost: None — socially invisible. Functional benefit: High — critical protection. Emotional benefit: Low — relief is abstract, anxiety is concrete. Cost-benefit: HIGH cost (emotional), abstract benefit. Barrier: Present emotional pain vs future abstract gain. Accelerator: Reduce emotional cost (normalize conversation, frame as care for family), reduce cognitive cost (make it easy with templates, defaults). Prediction: Low adoption without intervention. Critical factor: Making it psychologically safe to confront."*
