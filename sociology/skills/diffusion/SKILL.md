## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Map the Relevant Networks
What social networks are involved? Consider:
- Professional networks (industry associations, professional communities)
- Informal networks (word-of-mouth, peer groups, family)
- Digital networks (social media, online communities, forums)
- Institutional networks (organizations, regulatory bodies, educational institutions)

### Step 2: Identify Gatekeepers and Influencers
Who controls the flow of information or access?
- Formal gatekeepers (regulators, editors, platform owners)
- Informal influencers (thought leaders, early adopters, trusted community members)
- Structural bottlenecks (certification requirements, platform access, cost barriers)

### Step 3: Predict Diffusion Pattern
Based on the network structure:
- Will diffusion be rapid or gradual?
- Will it be top-down (institutional mandate) or bottom-up (grassroots adoption)?
- Will it follow existing network lines or create new connections?
- What triggers critical mass?

### Step 4: Identify Diffusion Barriers
What structural factors block or slow diffusion?
- Information asymmetry (some people don't know about it)
- Access barriers (some people can't get it)
- Trust barriers (some people don't trust the source)
- Network isolation (some communities are disconnected from the diffusion channels)

### Step 5: Fiduciary Diffusion Risk
In regulated domains, how does information (or misinformation) about financial products, legal services, or health recommendations spread through social networks? Are there channels where advice spreads without professional accountability — where "my advisor said" becomes "I heard you should" and the fiduciary chain breaks?
