## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Identify Affected Institutions
Which institutions are affected? Consider:
- Professions (what happens to the professional role, expertise, identity, and standards?)
- Organizations (how do organizational structures, cultures, and hierarchies change?)
- Regulatory frameworks (how does this interact with or challenge existing regulation?)
- Social norms (what behaviors become normalized or stigmatized?)

### Step 2: Analyze Institutional Change
For each affected institution:
- Is the institution being strengthened, weakened, transformed, or replaced?
- What functions does the institution currently serve, and are those functions still being served?
- Is the change happening from within or being imposed from outside?

### Step 3: Identify Institutional Resistance and Adaptation
- What resistance will this encounter from existing institutional structures?
- How might institutions adapt rather than be replaced?
- What new institutions might emerge?

### Step 4: Professional Identity and Accountability
If a profession is affected: How does this change the professional's identity, expertise, and accountability? When AI augments or replaces professional functions, what happens to the professional duty of care? Does the professional become a supervisor of AI (retaining accountability) or a conduit for AI (losing accountability)? This distinction has fiduciary implications in regulated domains.

### Step 5: Normative Shift
What becomes "normal" that wasn't before? What becomes "unacceptable" that was previously standard? How does this change what society expects?
