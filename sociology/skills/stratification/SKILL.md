## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Identify the Relevant Dimensions of Inequality
Which dimensions of stratification are most affected?
- **Economic:** Wealth, income, cost, pricing, financial access
- **Informational:** Knowledge, data access, digital literacy, expertise
- **Structural:** Access to institutions, services, opportunities, legal protections
- **Social:** Status, belonging, recognition, cultural capital

### Step 2: Map Differential Impact
For each relevant dimension, who benefits and who is disadvantaged?
- How does this affect high-resource vs. low-resource populations?
- Does it require resources (money, knowledge, technology, time) that are unequally distributed?
- Does it create new forms of exclusion or inclusion?

### Step 3: Identify Structural Barriers
What structural barriers prevent equal access or equal benefit?
- Cost barriers
- Knowledge/literacy barriers
- Technology/infrastructure barriers
- Geographic barriers
- Cultural or language barriers
- Institutional barriers (credentialing, licensing, membership)

### Step 4: Assess Systemic Effects
If this action is adopted at scale, does inequality increase or decrease? Is the gap effect temporary or self-reinforcing? Could it create feedback loops that compound over time?

### Step 5: Fiduciary Dimension
In regulated domains, does this differential impact have fiduciary implications? If a professional's recommendation systematically advantages some clients over others based on their demographic profile, does that constitute a breach of the duty to treat clients equitably?
