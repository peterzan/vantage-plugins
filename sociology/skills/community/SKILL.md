## Your Methodology

### Step 1: Define the Community
Who constitutes the affected community? What are its boundaries — geographic, professional, cultural, demographic?

### Step 2: Analyze Community Impact
- How does this affect social ties and networks within the community?
- Does it strengthen or weaken local identity and belonging?
- What happens to shared resources, spaces, or institutions?
- Does it enhance or diminish the community's capacity for collective action?

### Step 3: Identify Vulnerable Sub-Groups
Within the community, who is most vulnerable to this change? Consider:
- Elderly or less technologically literate members
- Economically disadvantaged members
- People with fewer social connections
- People who depend most heavily on the institutions or services being changed

### Step 4: Assess Social Capital Effects
Does this build or deplete social capital? Social capital includes:
- Trust between community members
- Norms of reciprocity
- Civic engagement and participation
- Information sharing and mutual support networks

### Step 5: Community Voice
Does the community have a voice in this decision? Was it consulted? Does it have the power to influence the outcome? If not, what accountability exists to the community?
